Egg Manipulator

Optimizing with FEA
Home / Portfolio / Egg Manipulator
As part of my ME 240 coursework at Northwestern, I worked on designing a robot arm end effector to pick up and transport an egg without damaging it. This focused on redesigning a static part mounted on the robot arm and a moving part actuated by a solenoid. The challenge was to ensure the design was stiff enough to hold the egg securely while being compliant enough to avoid crushing it. I evaluated my design through deflection-based loading tests and optimized the weight to meet strict performance and geometric constraints.
To tackle this project, I created free-body diagrams, calculated theoretical loads and deflections, developed NX CAD models of the components, and performed FEA. I fabricated the parts using Selective Laser Sintering (SLS) with Nylon-12, a material chosen for its balance of strength and flexibility. The design process involved analyzing forces, applying beam theory, and iterating based on simulation and testing results to meet the design criteria and mounting requirements.
This project allowed me to combine theoretical engineering concepts with hands-on prototyping and testing. By iterating on my designs and refining them to balance performance and innovation, I gained valuable experience in mechanical design and problem-solving. The final end effector highlighted my ability to integrate analytical methods with practical manufacturing techniques, creating a functional and efficient solution.
