Cast Iron Mugs

Home / Portfolio / Molding HSAs
I designed and created a set of cast iron mugs as part of DSGN 376 at Northwestern. Using SolidWorks, I began by modeling the mug based on dimensions from a friend’s favorite mug, incorporating ergonomic features to enhance its usability. To personalize each mug as a gift, I designed tessellating geometric patterns inspired by my friends’ unique personalities.

A key challenge was accounting for feasible feature sizes in the casting process, which required balancing intricate design elements with the limitations of molding and casting techniques. I then designed a mold by subtracting the mug models from a larger mold block and incorporating essential features like parting lines, sprues, and gates to ensure proper metal flow. After iterative feedback and collaboration with my instructor, I refined the design to improve its manufacturability and aesthetic detail.
After finalizing the design, we sent the molds to be 3D printed in sand. The iron pour itself was a school-wide collaboration held over an entire weekend at Evanston Township High School. We began by cleaning and assembling the molds and carefully binding them together to prepare for casting. Once the forge was fired up, we smelted scrap iron and poured it into the molds. The experience was both thrilling and educational, as we also had the opportunity to engage with high school students, introducing them to STEM concepts and sparking their interest in engineering and design. After the metal cooled and the molds had cured, we broke them open to reveal our cast pieces. To transform the rough castings into functional mugs, I removed the gates and vents, then refined the surfaces using a dremel tool and sandblaster. Finally, I seasoned the mugs like cast iron pans with oil, giving them a protective finish. The result was a set of beautiful, heavy-duty mugs that are perfect for cozy winter days—albeit a bit hefty to handle!
